## Laboratory Assignments

CPSC 220 has a sequence of modules.  Each module has lectures and labs.  Labs consist of questions, several programming problems (or individual labs) that allow you to practice the concepts from the lectures, and potentially a programming contest problem.  I lead some of the lab programming problems - we create the solution together.  You can follow along with me or you can perform the lab at your own pace, asking questions as needed. You begin solving lab problems and writing code during our in-class period, and you complete the lab by its due date, which is typically a week after we begin.  The following are attributes of labs.  

* Attendance at in-class labs is mandatory unless you have made prior arrangements. 
* Each lab problem includes the number of points you can earn by completing the problem.
* The goal is for you to earn as many points as possible.
* Each lab problem must be completed with at least 1 point in order to pass the class.
* You assign points to yourself and you must demonstrate your work to a team member.
* For a lab that has a programming contest problem, a team must submit a solution for the problem for each member to earn their total lab points.  If the programming contest solution successfully solves the problem, team members are awarded additional lab points.
* The basic grade for the lab component of your grade is the number of points you earn divided by the total lab points.  At this time there are 1165 total lab points.  You can receive a higher grade for labs by demonstrating an honest commitment to completing all labs.  Labs are for learning, and I want you to learn.  For example, under the basic scoring you would get an 88 if you earned 1025 out of 1165 points, but you can earn 100 for the lab component by earning 1025 and demonstrating an honest commitment to completing all labs.
* All labs must be completed by due dates, which are posted on Canvas.  Because topics and course projects build on information covered in laboratory exercises, it is in your best interest to complete the labs on time.
* Labs submitted after the due date must be discussed with me for the total points.
* [Lab submissions](/gustycooper.github.io/labs_lab00_00) describes the requirements for submitting labs on Canvas.
